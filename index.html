About

My work connects methodological advances in optimization and machine learning with operational decisions in freight, logistics, maritime, public transport, and shared mobility systems.

Current Appointment

Research Identity

Transport optimization under uncertainty

My research focuses on multimodal and synchromodal planning, vehicle routing, ship and maritime logistics, preference-aware optimization, and data-driven transport operations.

Academic Training

Delft-trained in transport engineering and logistics

I received my Ph.D. from Delft University of Technology under Prof. Rudy R. Negenborn and Dr. Bilge Atasoy, and later worked with Prof. Oded Cats and Dr. Shadi Sharif Azadeh on sustainable multimodal passenger transport.

Research

The common thread is decision intelligence for transport systems where demand, service time, capacity, preferences, and collaboration are all uncertain and dynamic.

Synchromodal Freight

Dynamic rail-water-road planning

Re-planning, flexible services, shipper preference modeling, and collaborative routing for intermodal freight networks.

AI and OR

Learning-assisted optimization

Hybrid reinforcement learning, adaptive large neighborhood search, stochastic optimization, and model-assisted multi-agent control.

Passenger Mobility

Public transport and shared mobility

Preference-based trip planning, mixed passenger-parcel services, shared fleets, rebalancing, and multimodal passenger decisions.

Maritime Logistics

Data-driven ship and port operations

AIS-based mining, fleet routing, ship emissions, semantic behavior modeling, berth allocation, and collaborative port optimization.

Low-Altitude Transport

Air-ground logistics systems

Drone-integrated emergency logistics, aircraft-ground vehicle coordination, and joint passenger mobility with parcel delivery.

Sustainable Decisions

Cost, time, emissions, and preferences

Multi-objective methods that make trade-offs transparent for operators, shippers, passengers, and public agencies.
